Introduction

The OpenEyes Project Board asked us to evaluate the pros and cons of a temporary pause in new development work so that we can address some of the technological constraints.

We recommended a seven week Technical Proof of Concept (PoC) to prove or disprove five technical concepts which we believe could be key to the future of OpenEyes, and formulate platform migration options.

Benefits new architecture enables

Scalability of the development team
Enabled by a service layer architecture.
Development speed
Increased by de-coupling modules of functionality and developing in Java based language JVM such as Scala; furthermore by Angular as a front-end.
Workflow, Clinical scalability, Configurability and Deployment in multiple organisations

Workflow

We have demonstrated this architecture through a patient journey that is not well represented in OpenEyes today: a patient with Age Related Macular Degeneration.

This patient accounts for 60% of patients at the Medical Retina Injection suite. Over the course of their care during a visit, the patient meets several different clinicians at the following steps:

  • Registration
  • Vision Assessment
  • OCT Scan
  • Assessment for AMD
  • Injection
